# LawLens

## Overview

LawLens is an AI-powered legal intelligence platform designed to provide accurate, explainable, and citation-backed legal information using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).

The goal is to build a trustworthy legal AI assistant that answers legal questions using official government legislation instead of relying solely on Large Language Models.

LawLens focuses on reducing hallucinations through retrieval, evidence verification, jurisdiction-aware routing, and citation generation.

The platform is intended to evolve into a production-grade SaaS application rather than a prototype or college project.

---

# Project Goals

The primary objectives of LawLens are:

- Build a modern legal intelligence platform.
- Minimize hallucinations in legal question answering.
- Support multiple legal jurisdictions.
- Always ground responses using official legal documents.
- Provide transparent citations for every answer.
- Make legal information understandable for non-lawyers.
- Build a scalable architecture suitable for commercial deployment.
- Produce a research-quality implementation suitable for publication.

---

# Problem Statement

Large Language Models frequently generate inaccurate legal information because they rely on learned patterns instead of verified legal sources.

Incorrect legal advice can have serious real-world consequences.

LawLens solves this problem by retrieving relevant legislation before generating answers and by verifying every response against retrieved evidence.

# AI Principles

Every response generated by the system must follow these principles:

1. Retrieve relevant legal documents before generating an answer.
2. Never answer solely from model memory.
3. Never fabricate laws or legal citations.
4. Every factual claim should be supported by retrieved evidence.
5. Every answer should include citations whenever possible.
6. If evidence is insufficient, clearly state that instead of guessing.
7. Always prioritize factual accuracy over conversational fluency.

---

# Supported Data Sources

Only official legal sources should be used whenever possible.

Examples include:

- Government legislation portals
- Official constitutions
- Statutes
- Regulations
- Official gazettes

Avoid relying on blogs, forums, unofficial summaries, or AI-generated legal content as primary sources.
